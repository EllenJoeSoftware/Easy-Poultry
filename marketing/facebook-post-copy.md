# Posting checklist

Before you hit publish:

- [ ] **Image is set** — Facebook compresses heavily without one. Use the square SVG (download as PNG) for feed; landscape for link previews.
- [ ] **First line hooks** — most users only see the first ~150 characters before "See more" cuts it off. Open with curiosity or a question, not the URL.
- [ ] **URL towards the end OR in the first comment** — Facebook deprioritises posts with links in the body text. Putting `easypoultry.co.za` in the first comment after publishing usually gets 30–50% more reach.
- [ ] **Tag your location** — geotag your farm or "South Africa" so it appears in local discovery.
- [ ] **Tag relevant pages** — National Poultry Association SA, your local agri group, suppliers you're partnered with.
- [ ] **Reply to every comment within the first 30 minutes** — early engagement boosts the post's algorithmic reach for the next 24 hours.

# Reposting cadence

- **Week 1:** Post #1 (launch). Engage with every comment.
- **Week 2:** Post #4 (story). Personal voice = highest organic reach.
- **Week 3:** Post #2 (sellers). Drives shop sign-ups.
- **Week 4:** Post #5 (question). Drives engagement.
- **Ongoing:** Post #7 (auctions) every Monday evening before the Tuesday auction.

Mix in customer testimonials, listing screenshots, and behind-the-scenes farm photos in between.

# Free Facebook Group strategy

If you want even more free reach beyond your own page, share variations of these in:

- South African Poultry Farmers (group)
- Backyard Chickens South Africa (group)
- Indigenous Chickens / Boschveld breeder groups
- Your provincial agri groups (Eastern Cape Farmers, etc.)
- Local farm-to-table consumer groups

⚠️ **Read each group's rules first.** Some allow promo posts only on specific days. A genuine "we built a tool for our community" angle (like Post #4) almost always lands well; a hard sell almost never does.
